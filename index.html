DROUGHT

Awareness about drought is essential for individuals, communities, and governments, as drought can cause significant damage to the environment, economy, and human well-being. Drought is a prolonged period of abnormally low rainfall or dry conditions, which can lead to water scarcity, crop failure, and reduced availability of food and drinking water. Government agencies also have an important role to play in drought awareness and prevention. This can include monitoring water levels and issuing drought warnings, implementing water-use restrictions, and promoting water conservation and recycling programs. Governments can also invest in water infrastructure projects, such as dams and reservoirs, to increase water availability during times of drought.

FLOOD

Awareness about floods is crucial for individuals, communities, and governments, as floods can cause significant damage to infrastructure, property, and human life. Floods occur when water overflows from a river, lake, or ocean onto land that is normally dry, causing damage to homes, businesses, and other structures. Floods can occur at any time, and can be caused by a variety of factors, such as heavy rainfall, melting snow, storm surges, or dam failures.

AIR POLLUTION

Government agencies have an important role to play in air pollution awareness and prevention. This can include implementing air quality monitoring programs, enacting regulations to reduce emissions from industrial and transportation sources, and promoting clean energy and sustainable land use practices. Governments can also invest in public transportation infrastructure, promote electric vehicles and public transportation, and establish air quality standards to protect human health.

DEFORESTATION

Raising awareness about deforestation is critical to addressing this important environmental issue. Here are some ways to increase awareness:
1. Education: Educating people about the causes and consequences of deforestation is key to raising awareness. This can be done through schools, universities, NGOs, and media outlets.
2. Social media: Social media platforms provide a powerful tool for raising awareness about deforestation. People can share information, photos, and videos to highlight the issue and encourage action.
3. Advocacy: Advocacy efforts can help to influence policy decisions and promote action on deforestation. This can involve writing to elected officials, signing petitions, and participating in rallies and other events.
4. Sustainable consumer choices: Encouraging people to make sustainable consumer choices, such as purchasing products made from sustainably sourced wood or supporting companies that engage in sustainable practices, can also help to reduce deforestation.
5. Supporting conservation organizations: Supporting organizations that work to conserve forests and promote sustainable land use practices is another way to increase awareness and address deforestation.

BIO MASS

Biomass can help keep the environment clean by providing a renewable source of energy that produces fewer greenhouse gas emissions than fossil fuels. Biomass can come from a variety of sources, including agricultural waste, forestry residues, and dedicated energy crops. When these materials are burned to generate energy, they release carbon dioxide, but the amount released is offset by the carbon dioxide absorbed during the growth of the biomass feedstock. To raise awareness about the benefits of biomass, it's important to educate people about the environmental and economic advantages of using biomass energy. This can be done through a variety of channels, such as public information campaigns, educational programs in schools and universities, and outreach to community groups and local leaders. It's also important to address any concerns or misconceptions that people may have about biomass energy, such as worries about deforestation or competition with food crops. Effect of E WATSE

Here are some of the negative impacts of e-waste on the environment:
1. Soil contamination: When e-waste is dumped in landfills, it can contaminate the soil with toxic substances like lead, cadmium, and mercury. These chemicals can leach into the groundwater and pollute nearby water sources.
2. Air pollution: Burning e-waste releases toxic fumes into the air, including dioxins and furans, which can cause respiratory problems and other health issues.
3. Water pollution: E-waste can also pollute waterways and oceans when it is dumped into bodies of water or when toxic substances leach into groundwater.
4. Wildlife harm: Animals can be harmed by e-waste when they ingest it or get entangled in wires and cables.
To raise awareness about the negative impacts of e-waste, it is important to educate people about the importance of proper disposal of electronic waste. Here are some ways to do this:
1. Information campaigns: Public information campaigns can be used to raise awareness about the environmental and health impacts of e-waste and to provide information about proper disposal methods.
2. Recycling programs: Local governments can establish e-waste recycling programs to encourage proper disposal of electronic devices. These programs can include drop-off locations and curbside pickup.
3. Education and outreach: Schools and universities can provide education and outreach programs to teach students about the importance of e-waste recycling and proper disposal.
4. Corporate responsibility: Manufacturers can take responsibility for the end-of-life disposal of their products by implementing take-back programs or designing products with recyclability in mind.

Schemes Launched by the Government of India
1-NAMAMI GANGE


Launched in 2014, Namami Gange Programme is an Integrated Conservation Mission with a budget outlay of Rs. 20,000 crores. Key Achievements:
1- Collection of floating solid waste from the surface of ghats and rivers.
2- Biodiversity Conservation and Ganga Rejuvenation.
3- Creation of Sewerage treatment capacity.
4- Construction of toilets in Gram Panchayats of 5 Ganga Basin States.
5- Industrial Effluent Monitoring.

2- Green Skill Development Programme (GSDP)


Formally launched on May 15, 2018, Green Skill Development Programme (GSDP) aims at training over 5.5 lakh workers in environment and forest sectors pan India. The programme will help in attaining Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs), Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), National Biodiversity Targets (NBTs), as well as Waste Management Rules (2016). Key Achievements:
1- Skilled trainees successfully as Biodiversity Conservationists (Basic Course).
2- Skilled trainees successfully as Para-taxonomists (Advance Course).
In May 2018, the then Union Minister for Environment, Forest, and Climate Change, Harsh Vardhan, said that around 2.25 people will be employed through GSDP by 2019 and around five lakh will be employed by 2021.
The pilot project was launched in June 2017 to preserve and restore the environment and create a sustainable future.

3- Jal Jeevan Mission

Launched in August 2019, Jal Jeevan Mission envisages a supply of 55 litres of water per person per day to every rural household through Functional Household Tap Connections (FHTC) by 2024. Rs 3,50,000 crore will be spent on 1592 stressed blocks in 256 districts. The fund sharing pattern between the Centre and states is 90:10 for Himalayan and North-Eastern States, 50:50 for other states, and 100% for Union Territories.
Key Achievements:
Local infrastructure for rainwater harvesting, groundwater recharge and management of household wastewater for reuse.
It is a community-based approach to water and includes extensive Information, Education and Communication as key components of the mission.

4- Swachh Bharat Abhiyan


Launched on October 2, 2014, on the birth anniversary of Mahatma Gandhi, Swachh Bharat Abhiyan aims to eradicate open defecation and improve solid management.
Key Achievements:
1- 26,563 Swach Bharat Activities done.
2- 84,720 Swach Bharat Pledges taken.
3- 6,106 Swach Bharat Pakhwada Activities done.
4- Around 110 million toilets constructed between 2014 and 2019.

5- Atal Bhujal Yojana (ABHY)


Launched on December 25, 2019, on the 95th birth anniversary of former PM Atal Bihari Vajpayee, Atal Bhujal Yojana aims to improve groundwater management in 7 Indian states over a period of 5 years.
Key Achievement: The scheme is implemented in various districts of seven priority states-- Gujarat, Haryana, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Rajasthan and Uttar Pradesh.
The scheme is being implemented by the Ministry of Jal Shakti (earlier known as Ministry of Water Resources, River Development and Ganga Rejuvenation) and is funded by GOI and the World Bank on a 50:50 basis.

6-National Clean Air Programme

Launched in January 2019, aims to reduce air pollution by at least 20-30% in the next five years, with 2017 as its base year. The mission aims at reducing the concentration of coarse (PM10) and fine particles (PM2.5) by at least 20% by 2024. However, NGT has directed MoEFCC to reduce the aforementioned timeline and to increase the target of reduction. Key Achievements: 1- Implementation of the city-specific action plans. 2- Integrated Command and Control Centres (ICCCs) in smart cities. 3- Shift to CNG vehicles. It is to be noted that the Right to Clean Air is a part of the Right to Life and failure to address air pollution is a denial of the Right to Life under Article 21.
